t-SNE preserves local structure (neighborhood relationships). Points that are close in high-D stay close in 2D, but global distances are not meaningful.
UMAP preserves more global structure and tends to produce tighter, more separated clusters. It is also generally faster than t-SNE.

Both are non-linear dimensionality reduction techniques ideal for visualizing clusters in high-dimensional data. Neither should be used for quantitative distance measurements in the embedding.
