Why do gradients vanish?
During backpropagation, gradients are multiplied through each layer. Sigmoid saturates with a max derivative of 0.25, so gradients shrink by ~75% per layer — after 10 layers, the gradient is < 0.001% of the output gradient. ReLU has a derivative of exactly 1 for positive inputs, preserving gradient flow (though ~50% of neurons “die” with zero gradient). Weight initialization matters too: Xavier keeps variance stable for sigmoid/tanh, while He initialization is designed for ReLU’s half-active nature.

Why schedule the learning rate?
A large LR helps escape saddle points early but causes oscillation near the optimum. Scheduling gradually reduces LR for smooth convergence. Cosine Annealing provides a gentle decay curve. Warmup prevents instability from large early gradients. One Cycle ramps up then down, often reaching lower final loss in fewer epochs.
