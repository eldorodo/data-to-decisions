COMPAS Case Study
The COMPAS recidivism algorithm was studied by ProPublica (2016). It was simultaneously:
  • Calibrated (equal accuracy across races)
  • But violated Equal Opportunity (Black defendants had 2× FPR)
This is mathematically unavoidable when base rates differ between groups.
1. Demographic Parity
P(Ŷ=1 | A=0) = P(Ŷ=1 | A=1)
Equal approval rates across groups. Ignores whether each prediction is correct.
2. Equal Opportunity
P(Ŷ=1 | Y=1, A=0) = P(Ŷ=1 | Y=1, A=1)
Equal TPR (recall) for qualified applicants across groups.
3. Equalized Odds
Equal TPR AND FPR across groups
Stricter — requires both qualified AND unqualified applicants to be treated equally.
4. Calibration
P(Y=1 | Ŷ=p, A=a) = p for all groups a
Score means the same thing for all groups: a 70% score = 70% actual positive rate.
Impossibility theorem: When base rates differ across groups, Calibration and Equalized Odds cannot both be satisfied. You must choose — and document your choice.
